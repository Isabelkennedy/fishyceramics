The Method

The Kennedy
Wiggle.

A Acknowledge

Notice what is actually present — in the body, in the room, in the decision. Not what should be there. What is.

B Bring it closer

Don't manage it away. Get curious. The signal carries information — the discomfort, the resistance, the pull toward something.

C Do something with it

Move from awareness into response. Grounded, considered, coming from the whole of you — not just the part that's been performing.

Clay as Wellness
"I look at a flower and become completely absorbed — how many petals, how many fronds. From that fixation, I put it into clay. The piece represents true presence and nature frozen in time."

Clay is one of the oldest healing materials there is. It slows you down, brings you into your hands, and makes the present moment impossible to ignore. You cannot think your way through it. You have to feel it.

Sessions are built around pinch pots and freeform making — reacting to what arises, following what the hands want to do. The aim is to feel lighter and more present by the time you leave. No art experience needed. No outcome expected.

Available for individuals, small groups, and teams. Facilitated by Isabel — ceramicist, coach, and clay wellness practitioner.
